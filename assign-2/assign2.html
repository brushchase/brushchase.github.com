As real estate prices in Bushwick rise, residents strive to maintain housing affordability

By Chase Brush | November 19, 2018

With median rent prices rising almost 35 percent over the last eight years and home sale prices jumping a whopping 128 percent, Bushwick’s booming real estate market continues to be a boon for developers -- but also the source of grief for local residents and activists who lament the neighborhood’s growing unaffordability.

According to data by the NYC-based real estate agency StreetEasy, the median asking rent in Bushwick was $2,584.90 in 2018, up from $1,937.20 in 2010. Median home sale prices have also skyrocketed, rising from $399,695.92 eight years ago to $914,148 today.

The trends have spurred local activists and advocates into action. Earlier this year, Bushwick’s community board 4 unveiled the Bushwick Community Plan, a resident-generated framework for tackling out-of-character development and rising costs of living in the neighborhood.

Alex Fennell, the Network Director for the nonprofit Churches United For Fair Housing, said the plan's measures are intended to stave off the kind of out-of-control development that has overtaken Williamsburg, Bushwick’s neighbor to the west.

“It was presumed that the neighborhood wouldn’t change that much,” she said. “But the results are clear: a neighborhood that used to be 95 percent latino is now 95 percent white.”

“So that effect -- the effects of that gentrification, that speculation -- have rippled out into other neighborhoods, but Bushwick was one of the first affected neighborhoods.” she added.

Fennell noted that over 50 percent of residents in Bushwick are rent-burdened, and only ⅓ of residents live in rent-stabilized housing.
